# D9 — Baseline Backlog Reconciliation (Riley capability audit, 2026-06-10)

**Baseline:** `docs/audits/2026-06-02-riley-improvement-audit/FINDINGS.md` (written against `main` @ 38f0129d, post-#792). **Reconciled against:** current `main`-tracking worktree @ 84083f0c. Every status below was verified by reading current code, not the shipped-PR list.

## Thesis

Eight days after the baseline, its recommended sequence was followed with unusual fidelity: Tier 0 (floors, abstention, no-ghost test), Tier 1 (the eval gate), Tier 2 (economic targeting incl. the keystone), the highest-value Tier 3/5 wires (source economics → reallocation rec; Riley→Mira handoff), and a re-shaped Tier 7 first execution leg (governed pause, dark) are all real. **The baseline's meta-finding has, however, recurred one level up: the backlog's keystone shipped as code and is then discarded by configuration.** No producer anywhere writes `targetCostPerBooked`, `conversionActionType`, or `attributionWindows`, so every live deployment still judges on CPL with Meta's unfiltered aggregate denominator — the exact condition Tiers 0.1 and 2 existed to fix. "Computed then discarded" has become "shipped then unconfigured." The remaining open legs are the learning loop (enriched but flag-off and display-only), the `system_auto_approved` structural landmine, starved V2 analyzers, unchanged scale posture, and the conversational surface (deliberately deprioritized by the agent-panel redesign).

## What is sound (with evidence)

- **The measurement foundation is real and blocking.** The riley-recommendation eval runs the genuine `decideForCampaign` pipeline, covers abstention/tier/lockout/measurement-trust/durable-breach outcomes, and carries two meta-guards (denominator regression, fixture-coverage drift) — exactly the baseline's Tier-1 design, in CI (`ci.yml:536`).
- **The decision pipeline's safety architecture matches the spec it cites.** Gate-1 (measurement trust), Gate-2 (evidence floors), tier gating, and the dual learning-phase holds compose in a single readable function (`campaign-decision.ts:121-229`) with each gate's rationale documented inline.
- **The Phase-C execution spine honors every platform invariant it claims.** Seeded system principal, PlatformIngress submit, 4-segment idempotency key, `\"approvalRequired\" in response` branch, allow+mandatory-approval policy pair seeded atomically, capability-passing flag enforcement, audited flip script — each one is a named house gotcha, addressed by name in the code (`riley-pause-submit-request.ts`, `riley-pause-governance.ts`, `inngest-functions.ts:268-273`).
- **Failure paths degrade honestly.** Ad-set fetch failure → lead-share fallback → honest abstain with error-level log (`inngest-functions.ts:234-248`); outcome-row insert races are two-layer idempotent (`outcome-attribution.ts:371-384`); handoff failure never breaks the audit (`bootstrap/inngest.ts:361-365`).

## Verdict

The team executed the baseline's sequence nearly to the letter and at high quality. What remains is concentrated and nameable: **populate the economic config (D9-1), flip the outcome-attribution flag and close the learning leg (D9-5), and close the `system_auto_approved` structural hole (D9-2) before Spec-1B.** Those three, plus the alerting one-liner (D9-3), are the difference between "the backlog shipped" and "the backlog works in production."
