# R1. Market Landscape: AI Ad Creative and UGC Tools vs Mira

Part of the Mira capability audit. Research conducted 2026-06-10.

## Method and date

All research was performed on 2026-06-10 using web search plus targeted page fetches (Marketing Brew, icon.com, Singapore MOH, Meta Transparency Center, motionapp.com on Andromeda). A second pass the same day added Meta's Andromeda engineering metrics, HeyGen Avatar V dating, Malaysia MAB before/after policy, and Meta's CTWA optimization guidance. Claims rest on vendor pages, primary policy/regulatory sources, trade-press articles, and review/agency sites. Review-site pricing lags vendor changes and is marked where sources disagree. Stats that originate from agency blogs or vendor marketing are marked as unverified. Inferences by the author are labeled "inference."

Mira's current state is taken as given from the audit brief: two pipelines (polished: trend, hook, script, storyboard, image, video, assembly; UGC: planning/scripting/production with provider routing across Kling and HeyGen plus ElevenLabs voice and Whisper, realism scoring, frame QA, likeness-consent records); briefs from a self-brief cron and Riley handoffs; taste memory from operator Keep/Pass gestures; governed publish to Meta with ads created PAUSED and human approval mandatory.

## The capability bar vs Mira (explicit gaps)

Each dimension: where the market bar sits, where Mira stands, and the gap.

**1. Iteration speed.** Bar: a finished UGC-style video in 2-5 minutes (Arcads, [ezugc.ai](https://www.ezugc.ai/blog/arcads-ai)); hook-variant clips in 5-10 seconds (MakeUGC, [makeugc.ai/pricing](https://www.makeugc.ai/pricing)); prompt-to-finished-video agents (HeyGen Video Agent, Sept 2025, [aitoolsbee](https://aitoolsbee.com/news/heygen-ai-video-generator-hits-100m-arr-offers-agent-workflows/)). Mira: a multi-stage Inngest pipeline (trend through assembly) plus mandatory human approval. End-to-end latency was not measured in this research and should be benchmarked by the codebase workstream. Gap: structural. Mira is slower by design; the risk is not the approval gate but slow regeneration of variants on already-approved concepts.

**2. Variant volume.** Bar: batch generation of dozens to hundreds of variants per operation (Arcads batch mode, [ezugc.ai](https://www.ezugc.ai/blog/arcads-ai)); 5-10 variants per product URL (Creatify, [superscale.ai](https://superscale.ai/alternatives/creatify/pricing)); agentic tools claiming 15-20 variations in minutes ([adstellar.ai](https://www.adstellar.ai/blog/ai-agent-for-advertising-campaigns)). Demand side: post-Andromeda playbooks recommend 8-15 conceptually distinct concepts per campaign with 2-3 variations each, or ~5 new concepts weekly ([303.london](https://www.303.london/blog/complete-guide-to-creative-diversity-for-meta-andromeda), [segwise.ai](https://segwise.ai/blog/meta-andromeda-update-creative-strategy-2026)); one agency claims brands testing 20+ new ads monthly see 65% higher ROAS (unverified agency stat, [jetfuel.agency](https://jetfuel.agency/metas-2026-algorithm-update-what-andromeda-changed-and-how-to-adapt-your-ads/)). Mira: the brief-to-creative flow as described produces single creatives per brief; there is no described batch axis (hook x actor x CTA permutation). Gap: large and the most quantifiable one. A clinic feeding Andromeda 2 creatives a month is structurally penalized regardless of quality.

**3. Performance feedback loops.** Bar: Pencil connects to live ad accounts and emits per-ad prediction scores ([mindofcontent](https://mindofcontent.com/outrank/ads-creative-ai)); Omneky generates from cross-platform performance data ([omneky.com](https://www.omneky.com/)); Motion attributes performance to hook type and concept ([motionapp.com](https://motionapp.com/blog/andromeda-impact-on-bfcm)); 2026 agentic operators close the loop through publish-monitor-regenerate ([superscale.ai](https://superscale.ai/learn/ad-creative-automation/)). All of these loop on platform metrics (CTR, CPA, ROAS). Mira: Riley handoffs carry ad-economics context into briefs, and taste memory captures operator Keep/Pass gestures, which are subjective judgments, not outcomes. No described mechanism attributes spend, conversation starts, or booked appointments back to a specific creative and into taste memory or the next brief. Gap: Mira is behind the point-tool bar on metric feedback today, while sitting on a structurally better signal (receipted bookings via Alex and WhatsApp) that none of these tools can reach. This is simultaneously the biggest gap and the biggest opportunity.

**4. Hook libraries and creative intelligence.** Bar: 12M+ searchable ad libraries with AI hook/theme extraction (Foreplay, MagicBrief, [admanage.ai](https://admanage.ai/blog/foreplay-vs-magicbrief)); GetHookd claims 65M+ Meta ads ([gethookd.ai](https://www.gethookd.ai/blog/foreplay-vs-atria-vs-magicbrief-vs-gethookd)); Arcads ships a hook generator trained on proven copy patterns ([creati.ai](https://creati.ai/ai-tools/arcads/)). Hook-pattern performance stats circulate (question hooks +35% engagement, problem-solution +41% conversions) but are unverified vendor/agency numbers ([github roundup](https://github.com/bklkuysp/ai-creative-analytics-tools)). Mira: a trend stage in the polished pipeline and operator taste memory with a cold-start problem (small N, one operator's taste). Gap: real. Mira has no mined corpus of winning aesthetic-vertical hooks; generic libraries exist but are horizontal and not compliance-filtered for SG/MY healthcare rules.

**5. Avatar realism.** Bar: HeyGen Avatar IV (mid-2025) and Avatar V (2026) are widely ranked the most photorealistic ([heygen.com](https://www.heygen.com/avatars/avatar-iv), [wavespeed.ai guide](https://wavespeed.ai/blog/posts/heygen-avatar-iv-complete-guide-2026/)); Arcads' 1,000+ actor library sets the breadth bar ([ezugc.ai](https://www.ezugc.ai/blog/arcads-ai)). Mira: routes UGC production across Kling and HeyGen, so it inherits the frontier rather than lagging it, and its realism scoring plus frame QA is a quality gate most point tools lack (point tools ship whatever renders; users report lip-sync glitches on Creatify, [gethookd review](https://www.gethookd.ai/learn/creatify-ai-reviews-pricing-plans-alternatives-is-this-ugc-ai-video-generator-worth-it/)). Gap: none on realism access; Mira lacks actor/persona breadth (no described library of consented synthetic personas to permute across variants).

**6. Compliance tooling.** Bar: low everywhere. Point tools offer essentially no healthcare-specific compliance features; the market's answer to compliance anxiety is Icon selling human-filmed ads ([icon.com](https://icon.com/)). Meta enforces policy reactively at review time, and medspa advertisers report bans triggered by before/after imagery, syringes, and words like "injectable" ([deangarland.com](https://www.deangarland.com/blog/navigating-metas-advertising-changes-for-med-spas/)). Mira: likeness-consent records, ads created PAUSED, mandatory human approval, audited publish. This is ahead of every point tool surveyed. Gap inside the strength: nothing described in Mira lints creative content against the actual rule set (Meta health/wellness standards, Singapore HCSA advertisement regulations, Malaysia MAB requirements) before a human sees it. The rules are concrete and encodable; see next section.

**7. Pricing and unit cost.** Bar: ~$11 per finished UGC video (Arcads, [eesel.ai](https://www.eesel.ai/blog/arcads-ai-pricing)); $19-119/mo tools; $399/mo for 6 human-filmed ads (Icon, [icon.com](https://icon.com/)); model-layer cost favors Kling for volume (~6 credits/clip vs 40-70 for Sora 2/Veo 3.1, [alici.ai](https://alici.ai/blog/best-ai-video-ad-creators-2026)). Mira: COGS per finished creative (Kling/HeyGen renders, ElevenLabs, Whisper, retries from QA failures) was not benchmarked in this research. Gap: unknown; needs a unit-economics measurement. Kling-heavy routing for volume variants with HeyGen reserved for hero spots matches the market's cost logic (inference).

## Medspa creative patterns (Meta and click-to-WhatsApp, 2025-2026)

**The US medspa playbook.** Agencies converge on: video over static; the first 2-3 seconds decide everything; 3-5 creatives per ad set rotated continuously; benefit-forward copy that opens with offer, reputation, and location; trust assets (practitioner credentials, review ratings) featured in creative ([medspamagicmarketing 2025 series](https://medspamagicmarketing.com/tips-advice/meta-ads-2025/), [clinicgrower](https://clinicgrower.com/med-spa-facebook-ads/), [wellstreetmedia 2025-07](https://wellstreetmedia.com/2025/07/how-to-run-successful-meta-ads-for-a-med-spa/)). Creative that trips Meta's automated review: before/after photos, syringe imagery, the word "injectable" in primary text ([deangarland.com](https://www.deangarland.com/blog/navigating-metas-advertising-changes-for-med-spas/)).

**Meta policy (primary source).** Cosmetic procedure ads must target 18+. General cosmetic before/after transformations are allowed only without negative-self-perception tactics. Anti-aging and wrinkle treatments (the core medspa categories: botox, fillers) may show zoomed/close-up results that "must reflect realistic outcomes" and "should not include side-by-side comparisons." Weight loss ads cannot show side-by-side transformations or fat-pinching close-ups. No ad may imply negative self-perception or a perfect body ideal ([Meta Transparency Center, health and wellness](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/health-wellness/), fetched 2026-06-10).

**Meta health-category data restriction.** Since January-February 2025, advertisers categorized as health and wellness lose lower-funnel web event optimization (conversions, add-to-cart) and conversion-based audiences ([triplewhale.com](https://www.triplewhale.com/blog/meta-health-and-wellness-brands), [admetrics.io](https://www.admetrics.io/en/post/metas-2025-health-wellness-advertising-restrictions)). Inference, marked: click-to-WhatsApp conversation events are on-platform engagement events rather than restricted web pixel events, which makes conversation-optimized CTWA one of the few full-strength optimization paths left for categorized clinics. This favors Switchboard's architecture but should be verified against Meta's current categorization behavior for actual pilot ad accounts.

**Singapore law makes much of the global playbook illegal.** Under the Healthcare Services Act 2020 and Healthcare Services (Advertisement) Regulations 2021, licensed providers' ads must be "accurate, truthful and do not induce unnecessary consumption," and unlicensed persons (including influencers) cannot advertise licensable services; penalties reach SGD 20,000 and/or 12 months imprisonment ([MOH parliamentary reply, 2024-01-09](https://www.moh.gov.sg/newsroom/regulation-of-advertisements-for-aesthetic-treatments/), fetched 2026-06-10). Secondary legal summaries consistently state the regulations prohibit before/after photos in advertisements outright, prohibit testimonials/endorsements in ads (with a narrow exception for unaltered testimonials on the institution's own website and official accounts), and ban superlatives, comparative claims, and urgency tactics ([yangsweb HCSA guide](https://www.yangsweb.com/blog/moh-phmc-advertising-guidelines), [thehealthcollective.sg](https://www.thehealthcollective.sg/articles/phmc-hcsa-advertising-regulations), [MOH HCSA advertisement regulations FAQ PDF](<https://isomer-user-content.by.gov.sg/7/a2d9019f-7617-40f1-8788-7e105b0a77e9/12-10-2023-advert-regs-faqs-(1).pdf>)). The exact regulation numbering (e.g., "Reg 5(1)(d)") is taken from secondary sources and should be confirmed against the statute text before encoding. Consequence: the dominant AI-UGC product shape (synthetic customer giving a testimonial with a transformation reveal) is legally hazardous for Singapore clinic ads, not just Meta-policy hazardous.

**Malaysia requires pre-approval.** Aesthetic services are medical treatment and may be advertised only by licensed/registered clinics with Medicine Advertisements Board approval; approved ads carry a KKLIU number, with approval issued within about 5 working days of a complete application ([pharmacy.moh.gov.my FAQ](https://pharmacy.moh.gov.my/en/faq/advertisement-aesthetic-services-allowed.html), [MAB advertising guidelines for healthcare facilities PDF](https://pharmacy.moh.gov.my/sites/default/files/document-upload/advertising-guidelines-healthcare-facilities-and-services-mab-3.2023.pdf)). MAB policy also states explicitly that an advertisement "should not contain 'before' and 'after' picture" ([MAB policy and decisions on services PDF, dated 2025-05-15](https://pharmacy.moh.gov.my/sites/default/files/document-upload/15.5.2025-mab-policy-and-decision-services.pdf)), so the before/after prohibition holds in both jurisdictions. An ad pipeline serving MY clinics needs a pre-approval workflow step, not just a publish gate.

**What compliant winning creative looks like for SG/MY clinics (synthesis, inference).** Provider-led expert videos (the doctor or therapist explaining a treatment), educational and behind-the-scenes formats, natural-results framing without comparisons, offer plus location plus credentials in the opening line, and CTWA-native design: benefit-led copy written around the conversation ("Chat with us on WhatsApp to check suitability"), CTA buttons that say "Chat now" or "Message us" rather than "Learn More," WhatsApp UI cues or phone-in-hand visuals for trust, an instant contextual first reply, and creative rotation every 2-3 weeks ([gallabox CTWA guide](https://gallabox.com/blog/click-to-whatsapp-ads), [infobip CTWA guide](https://www.infobip.com/blog/click-to-whatsapp-ads), [asisteclick CTWA 2026 guide](https://asisteclick.com/en/blog/click-to-whatsapp-ads-ctwa-conversion-2026/), [WhatsApp Business best practices](https://business.whatsapp.com/resources/resource-library/whatsapp-business-app-resources/ads-that-click-to-whatsapp-best-practices)). On the buying side, Meta-aligned guidance is to use the Engagement objective optimized to Conversations so delivery targets likely chatters, let campaigns learn at least 7 days before changes, and send real outcome signals (bookings, not chat opens) back via Conversions API ([kommo CTWA guide](https://www.kommo.com/blog/click-to-whatsapp/), [infobip](https://www.infobip.com/blog/click-to-whatsapp-ads)). CTWA is reported as the fastest-growing channel within Meta Ads in WhatsApp-dominant markets including Southeast Asia ([asisteclick](https://asisteclick.com/en/blog/click-to-whatsapp-ads-ctwa-conversion-2026/)); trade reporting on Meta's Q3 2025 earnings put click-to-WhatsApp ad revenue growth at roughly 60% year over year ([storyboard18](https://www.storyboard18.com/advertising/metas-click-to-whatsapp-ads-surge-60-yoy-in-q3-cementing-messaging-as-next-big-revenue-driver-83392.htm), relayed figure, moderately confident).

## Implications for Mira (ranked)

1. **Close the receipts loop before adding generation features.** Every serious point tool now feeds platform metrics back into generation (Pencil, Omneky, Motion, the 2026 agentic operators), and Meta itself loops at CTR/conversion level. Mira's only structural advantage over all of them is the signal it can own: spend to WhatsApp conversation to Alex-booked, receipted appointment, per creative. Today Mira's taste memory learns from Keep/Pass gestures, which is operator taste, not outcomes. Wiring per-creative outcome attribution into briefs and taste memory converts Mira from a slower point tool into the only creative agent optimizing on revenue. Without it, Mira is behind the bar on feedback, full stop.

2. **Encode compliance as a pipeline gate, not a human's job.** The rule set is concrete: Meta health/wellness standards (no side-by-side before/after for wrinkle treatments, no negative self-perception, 18+), Singapore HCSAR (no before/after in ads, no testimonials in ads, no superlatives or urgency), Malaysia MAB pre-approval with KKLIU numbering. No surveyed tool lints any of this; clinics get banned by trial and error. A compliance-lint stage before human approval (and an MY pre-approval workflow) makes Mira's existing governance (consent records, PAUSED publish, mandatory approval) into the product's sharpest commercial edge for licensed clinics. It also constrains generation away from the testimonial/transformation formats that generic UGC tools are optimized for and that SG law prohibits.

3. **Raise variant volume to Andromeda norms via permutation, not more pipelines.** Post-Andromeda guidance converges on 8-15 distinct concepts per campaign with 2-3 variations each, refreshed weekly. The market does this with batch axes: same script with permuted hooks, actors, CTAs, openings (Arcads batch mode; Creatify's 5-10 per URL). Mira's pipeline produces ones, not tens. Add a variant-fanout step (hook x persona x CTA) on top of an approved concept so volume comes after approval, keeping the governance cost per variant near zero.

4. **Build the vertical hook library Mira's taste memory cannot cold-start alone.** Generic tools mine 12M-65M horizontal ads; none filters for aesthetic-vertical, CTWA-format, SG/MY-compliant patterns. Seed a compliance-filtered library from Meta Ad Library mining of aesthetic clinics in WhatsApp-dominant markets plus Mira's own outcome data (implication 1), and use it to drive the trend/hook stages. This is a durable data asset; Keep/Pass alone is one operator's taste with small N.

5. **Make CTWA-native creative a first-class brief type.** The winning CTWA pattern is specific: conversation-designed copy, "Chat now" CTA matching, WhatsApp UI/phone-in-hand trust cues, and a contextual instant first reply that Alex already provides. Mira's brief schema and assembly stage should know an ad's destination is a conversation, not a landing page. This is cheap and directly aligned with the SG/MY no-PMS WhatsApp wedge.

6. **Hold the authenticity line; it is becoming the differentiator.** Icon pivoted from "first AI Admaker" to selling human-filmed ads labeled "100% real / not AI," and agencies tell Marketing Brew they hesitate to use gen-AI creative and see worse results from Meta's native tools. Mira's realism scoring, frame QA, and likeness-consent records are the right posture; extend them with an explicit disclosure stance and a hybrid path that assembles real clinic/provider footage (provider-led formats are also the compliant ones per implication 2).

7. **Benchmark unit cost against the $11 video.** Arcads sells finished UGC videos at ~$11 each; Kling is the volume-cost model (~6 credits/clip vs 40-70 for Sora 2/Veo 3.1 on one wrapper). Measure Mira's all-in COGS per shipped creative including QA retries, and bias provider routing (Kling for volume variants, HeyGen for hero creatives) accordingly. Uncertain until measured.
