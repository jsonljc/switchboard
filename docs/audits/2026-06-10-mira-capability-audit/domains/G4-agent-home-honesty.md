The desk renders the brief box plus honest empty copy per module (desk-copy.ts:39-46), the panel says "No drafts waiting" (mira-panel.tsx:31-32), metrics are zeros, wins and activity are empty lists. ## Findings

### G4-F1 (P1, verified): once any pipeline death occurs, the live desk narrates it as perpetual work: working masthead, animated drafting sprite, ageless present-progressive tray copy, and a greeting that invites review of a dead draft

Claim: the desk's working signal and tray labels have no staleness or failure honesty, so the D5-F1 zombie population makes Mira claim active drafting forever, and a with-video zombie becomes the greeting's named invitation forever.

Evidence: working = inProduction.length > 0 under the comment "Honest working signal" (mira-desk-page.tsx:35-44); deriveDeskItemState maps in_progress, video-less awaiting_review, AND "failed" to "in_production" (desk-model.ts:67-71); tray rows render only title plus STAGE_COPY/UGC_PHASE_COPY ("Drafting", "Filming the clip", desk-copy.ts:10,19) with no timestamp (mira-in-production-tray.tsx:60-70); with-video zombies count as ready_to_review and become "[title] is ready for your review" (desk-model.ts:68, greeting.ts:204-208, via oldestReadyToReview at prisma-greeting-signal-store.ts:106-120). Counter-evidence checked: pipeline tiles do carry a relative age but have zero UI consumers; the tray's problem chip surfaces only mapped failures (production-stage errors, ugcFailure), which D5-F1 zombies never set; no stalled-creative sweep exists (D5-F1).

Impact on the north star: the desk is the pilot's daily trust surface; an operator who watches "Drafting" for three days with the sprite animating, then learns the job died on day one, stops believing every other claim on the desk, including the honest ones.

Recommendation: ride the D5-F1 terminal-marker fix and add the narrative leg in the same slice: exclude problem-flagged items from the working signal, render updatedAt-based age in the tray, and switch any in_production item older than a threshold (say 6h, renders finish in minutes) to stalled copy ("This one's stuck. I'll need a nudge or a stop.") instead of present-progressive. Tag: extends (D5-F1/D9-F2 recommendation, narrative layer added). Effort: S.

### G4-F2 (P2, verified): Mira's activity feed is structurally empty forever, the translator has zero creative vocabulary, and any future agent-typed creative audit entry without an agentRole stamp will misattribute to Alex

Claim: no producer can ever write an AuditEntry the activity route attributes to Mira, so GET /agents/mira/activity returns [] regardless of how much Mira does; and the feed's classifier would misdescribe creative events as conversation if attribution ever arrived.

Evidence: the deps query filters actorType "agent" (cockpit-activity-deps.ts:48-49); matching requires actorId === "mira" or snapshot.agentRole === "mira" (cockpit-activity-translator.ts:30-43); every Mira submit uses actor {id:"system",type:"system"} (mira-self-brief-request.ts:45,77) or {id, type:"user"} (mira-brief.ts:136); repo-wide grep finds no writer of snapshot.agentRole (only readers) and no actorId "mira". The delegate tool writes agent-typed entries with a UUID id (delegate.ts:77 `actor: { id: ctx.actorId ?? ctx.deploymentId, type: "agent" }`), which the legacy fallback hands to Alex when agentRole is absent (translator:39). classify() has no creative.\* rule and defaults to "replied" (translator:66-68), whose contact-less head is "Reply sent" (translator:126). cockpit-activity-translator.test.ts contains no Mira case.

Impact: latent today (no Mira activity proxy/UI), but the planned agent-panel 4-slot parity will open this feed onto a permanently blank or, worse, Alex-leaking surface, and the "what did Mira do" question stays unanswerable outside the desk.

Recommendation: define the attribution convention now: stamp snapshot.agentRole at ingress audit-write time from the deployment's canonical agent key, and add creative.\* KIND_RULES (drafted, rendered, kept, published-paused) before any Mira feed ships. Tag: extends (D7 open question 3 named the trust shape; 4-slot panel is the planned consumer). Effort: M.

### G4-F3 (P2, verified): "Drafts completed" counts gesture recency, not completion, and two unflagged writers inflate it today, extending D5-F6's latent finding into the live present

Claim: reviewing old drafts this week adds them to this week's "Drafts completed" hero and to the voice line "+N drafts completed vs last week", because the Keep/Pass write and the taste-capture sweep both bump CreativeJob.updatedAt, the week-bucket key.

Evidence: shippedThisWeek = draft_ready with updatedAt >= weekStart (build-read-model.ts:56-63); the decision route writes the row directly (mira-decision.ts:59-62 `updateMany ... data: { reviewDecision: decision, reviewDecidedAt ... }`), the taste sweep writes it again (prisma-creative-job-store.ts:247 `data: { tasteCapturedAt: observedDecidedAt }`), and `updatedAt DateTime @updatedAt` bumps on both (schema.prisma:1428; Prisma applies @updatedAt to updateMany). Consumers: hero and stat cell "Drafts completed" plus voiceText delta (metrics-mira.ts:12-19,36-47,65-69) and Mira's own self-assessment prompt "Shipped this week: ${counts.shippedThisWeek}" (builders/mira.ts:127). Double distortion: the bump also drains shippedPrevWeek (build-read-model.ts:64-69), so one re-touched draft swings the delta by two. Passed (rejected) drafts count as completed since isCompleted ignores reviewDecision (build-read-model.ts:56). D5-F6 cited the decision/taste writes but scoped the finding "latent today because the kill switch defaults off"; the decision and taste legs need no flag.

Impact: the only consumer live today is Mira's chat prompt, so Mira misstates her own throughput to the operator in conversation; the dashboard KPI joins the moment any Mira metrics consumer ships.

Recommendation: same fix D5-F6 proposed, persist completedAt at the terminal save and bucket weeks off it; additionally decide whether passed drafts belong in "completed" or the label becomes "drafts produced". Tag: extends (D5-F6). Effort: S.

### G4-F4 (P2, verified): the quiet greeting promises a notification that does not exist

Claim: "No drafts need you right now. I'll ping you when one's ready." (greeting.ts:177) overclaims proactivity: nothing notifies anyone when a draft reaches draft_ready.

Evidence: neither runner contains any alert/notify call (grep over creative-job-runner.ts, ugc-job-runner.ts: zero hits); the async-failure alerter is wired alert:false for creative runners (D5-F4); Mira creates no PendingActionRecord rows (prisma-greeting-signal-store.ts:88-90 "Mira has no pending-action rows"), so nothing lands in the operator inbox on completion; draft-ready surfaces only passively on the desk/feed. The welcome variant's other claims survive scrutiny (publish gating is real, D4). Counter-evidence checked: brief-approval parks do reach the inbox, but that is a request-to-spend ping at submit time, not the promised draft-ready ping.

Impact: small but corrosive copy dishonesty on the surface that defines Mira's voice; an operator who trusts the ping will not poll the desk and will find week-old drafts.

Recommendation: either soften the copy ("New drafts will be waiting on my desk") or wire a real notifier (the lifecycle respond fork's Slack/Telegram bridge is the natural target) on the terminal save. Tag: new. Effort: S for copy, M for the notifier.

Recommendation: thread hasVideo (or the derived awaitingGo) into MiraPipelineRow and label gates "Waiting for your go-ahead"; align stage classification (gates are arguably "hot", the label is the lie, not the heat). Tag: extends (slice-3 mode-honest labels spec). Effort: S.

## Verdict

On the surfaces an operator actually sees today (desk greeting, masthead, trays, mission, panel), Mira is honest about what she is and what she has not done, and her two boldest claims (draft-only role, never publishing alone) are structurally true; but she is not honest about death or proactivity, and her bookkeeping is not honest about time. One dead render makes the desk claim active drafting indefinitely with no age cue and the greeting invite review of a corpse (G4-F1, the operational twin of D5-F1); the quiet greeting promises a ping no code sends (G4-F4); and "Drafts completed" counts touches, not completions, live today through the Keep/Pass and taste writes (G4-F3, extending D5-F6). The remaining narrative surfaces (activity, wins, metrics tiles, pipeline strip) are UI-orphaned, which is the only reason their structural emptiness (G4-F2, G4-F6) and pre-wired mislabels (G4-F5) are not visible dishonesty yet; they must be fixed before the planned panel parity opens them. Net: trust the desk while everything is alive, do not trust it after the first silent failure, and fix the staleness narrative in the same PR as the D5-F1 terminal marker.
